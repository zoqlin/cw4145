# CW4145 Final Deck Narrative Plan

## Audience
UNNC HCI tutors and classmates evaluating the full mixed reality design process, implementation quality, and reflection quality.

## Objective
Show that the project is not only a visual demo, but a site-specific mixed reality performance experience grounded in research, iterative ideation, prototype debugging, user testing, and HCI reflection.

## Narrative Arc
1. Open with the final visual identity and one-sentence concept.
2. Show why the UNNC lawn matters as a spatial setting.
3. Explain how ideation evolved from fragile recognition to stable world-locked performance.
4. Demonstrate the final prototype composition and core interaction loop.
5. Show technical implementation, prototype evidence, and testing.
6. Close with HCI concepts, limitations, and why the final version is academically strong.

## Visual System
- Primary palette: champagne green, petal pink, deep graphite green, soft gold.
- Typography: serif-led titles for dramatic presentation, clean sans-serif body for academic readability.
- Layout mix: hero cover, split-feature slides, storyboard slides, metric/dashboard slides, and reflective closeout slides.
- Imagery: member cards and prototype assets are used as stage motifs and visual anchors.

## Asset Plan
- Prototype screenshots or recording stills should be swapped in if available.
- Site research photos and MR creative card photos should replace any placeholders before final submission if the user has them.
- Existing project assets are used to keep the deck visually coherent with the prototype.

## Editability Plan
- All titles, subtitles, labels, notes, and explanatory content remain editable PowerPoint objects.
- Decorative imagery is isolated in framed image regions so screenshots can be replaced later without rebuilding the whole deck.
